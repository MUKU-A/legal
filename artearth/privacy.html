ARtEarth Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 3, 2026
Last updated: May 3, 2026

This Privacy Policy ("Policy") describes how the individual developer of the ARtEarth mobile application ("ARtEarth", the "App", "we", "us", or "our") collects, uses, shares, and protects information in connection with your use of the App. By using ARtEarth you agree to the practices described in this Policy.

If you do not agree with this Policy, please do not use the App.

1. Operator and Contact

ARtEarth is operated by an individual developer based in Japan.

  • Contact: artearth.app.contact@gmail.com

For any questions, requests, or complaints regarding this Policy or your personal information, please contact us at the address above. We will respond in Japanese or English.

2. Scope

This Policy applies to information processed through the ARtEarth iOS and Android applications and any related backend services we operate. It does not apply to third‑party websites, services, or applications that may be linked from the App.

3. Information We Collect

We collect the following categories of information.

3.1 Account and profile information

  • Sign‑in identifiers obtained when you sign in with Apple or Google (e.g. an opaque user identifier, the email address you choose to share, and, for Apple, the name you choose to share on first sign‑in).
  • Profile data that you provide in the App: username, biography, avatar image, language preference (locale), profile visibility setting, and date of birth (used to confirm eligibility and to compute age‑appropriate experience).

3.2 User‑generated content (UGC)

  • 3D models you upload or create (GLB files, voxel models, 2D images), their thumbnails and automatically generated preview renders.
  • AR placements you create, including their coordinates (latitude, longitude, altitude offset), the resolved address, the associated AR Cloud Anchor identifier, and metadata you provide (title, description, tags).
  • Photos you take with the AR camera and upload to the App.
  • Paint strokes drawn with the in‑app AR paint feature.
  • Messages you send in proximity chat rooms.
  • Social signals: likes, follows, blocks, collections, and reports you submit about other users or content.

3.3 Location information

  • Precise location (GPS) is collected only while the App is in the foreground and only when location permission is granted. We use it to:
    • show nearby AR artworks on the map,
    • place and resolve AR works at your current location,
    • allow you to join proximity chat rooms within approximately 50 meters of a placement.
  • Location data tied to AR placements you publish becomes part of that placement's public metadata.

3.4 Camera, microphone, and photo library

  • Camera is used to render the AR experience, to host or resolve AR Cloud Anchors, and to capture photos within the App. Camera frames used for Cloud Anchor hosting/resolution are sent to our AR provider (see §6).
  • Microphone may be used when recording video alongside AR content.
  • Photo library access is requested when you choose a profile picture or save a captured photo to your device.

We do not continuously upload raw camera or microphone streams. Camera and microphone data are processed for the specific feature you invoke.

3.5 Device, log, and technical data

  • Device push notification tokens (used to deliver notifications you have enabled).
  • Device model, OS version, app version, language and region settings.
  • Server logs containing IP address, request timestamps, and similar technical information necessary to operate the service and detect abuse.
  • Crash and error diagnostics where applicable.

3.6 Advertising identifier (iOS)

If you use the free plan on iOS and grant App Tracking Transparency (ATT) permission, the Identifier for Advertisers (IDFA) may be made available to our advertising partner for the purpose of personalized advertising and attribution. If you do not grant ATT permission, advertisements are still shown but are not personalized using the IDFA. You can change your ATT choice at any time in iOS Settings.

3.7 Purchase information

When you make an in‑app purchase or subscribe through Apple's App Store, we receive a signed transaction receipt from Apple containing the product identifier, transaction identifier, original transaction identifier, purchase date, and signed payload. We do not receive your payment card details. Apple processes the payment on its own terms.

4. How We Use Information

We use the information described above to:

  • provide and operate the AR experience (rendering, anchor hosting and resolution, location‑based discovery);
  • enable social features such as likes, follows, proximity chat, and notifications;
  • grant entitlements for subscriptions and consumable purchases;
  • moderate user content and respond to reports (see §7);
  • prevent fraud, abuse, and security incidents;
  • deliver advertising on the free plan and measure attribution where permitted by ATT;
  • communicate with you about service updates, support, and announcements;
  • comply with applicable laws and lawful requests from authorities.

5. AI‑Generated Content

ARtEarth offers an optional AI generation feature that turns a text prompt into a 3D model. When you use this feature:

  • Your prompt and any related parameters are transmitted to third‑party AI service providers that we engage on a confidential basis to perform the generation.
  • The resulting 3D model is stored under your account in the same way as models you upload yourself and is subject to the same moderation rules.
  • We may retain prompts and outputs in operational logs for abuse prevention and to enforce monthly usage quotas.

We may change the underlying AI service providers at any time without individual notice. Sensitive personal information should not be entered as a prompt.

6. Content Moderation

User content placed in real‑world locations is subject to moderation:

  • automatic checks on uploaded models, including thumbnail rendering and classification,
  • manual review of content that is reported, flagged, or submitted under the Supersize program,
  • removal or hiding of content that violates our Terms of Service.

We retain reports, moderation decisions, and minimally necessary metadata of removed content for the purpose of recordkeeping and to prevent repeated violations.

7. How Information Is Shared

We share information only as described below.

7.1 With other users

The following items are visible to other users of the App by design:

  • your username and avatar (always shown to attribute your published works and chat messages), and — where your profile is set to public — your biography, artwork list, and follower count on your profile page;
  • artworks you publish and their AR placements, including coordinates, address, title, description, and tags;
  • photos you attach to placements;
  • messages you post in proximity chat rooms (visible to other participants);
  • likes and follow relationships, except where hidden by your privacy settings.

Your date of birth, email address, push tokens, IDFA, and purchase receipts are not shown to other users.

7.2 With service providers

We use the following categories of service providers, each of which receives only the information necessary to perform its function:

  • Backend platform (Supabase) — hosting of database, authentication, storage, realtime messaging, and serverless functions.
  • Apple Inc. — Sign in with Apple, App Store / StoreKit billing, and ARKit on‑device AR.
  • Google LLC — Google Sign‑In; Google Maps SDK and Geocoding API for map display and reverse geocoding; Google ARCore Cloud Anchors and Geospatial APIs for AR anchor persistence; Firebase Cloud Messaging for push notifications; Google AdMob for advertising on the free plan.
  • Third‑party AI service providers for the AI generation feature, as described in §5.
  • Operational tools for analytics, crash reporting, and customer support where applicable.

These providers process data on our behalf under their respective terms and privacy policies. We encourage you to review their policies for further detail.

7.3 Legal and safety disclosures

We may disclose information to authorities or third parties when we believe in good faith that disclosure is required by law, necessary to enforce our Terms of Service, or necessary to protect the rights, safety, or property of ARtEarth, its users, or the public.

7.4 Business transfers

If the App or its operations are transferred to a successor (for example, in the context of a sale of the service), user information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We will give reasonable notice before personal information becomes subject to a different privacy policy.

7.5 No sale of personal information

We do not sell your personal information.

8. International Data Transfers

Some of our service providers operate servers outside Japan (for example, in the United States). When personal information is transferred outside Japan, we rely on the legal frameworks and contractual safeguards offered by those providers, as permitted by Japan's Act on the Protection of Personal Information ("APPI").

9. Data Retention

We retain personal information for as long as it is necessary to provide the App to you, plus any additional period required by applicable laws (for example, retention obligations under Japanese tax, accounting, and consumer‑protection legislation, and limitations periods for legal claims).

When you delete your account from within the App, your account record and content owned by you are deleted or anonymized in our primary databases. Some information may persist for a limited additional period in encrypted backups, in moderation records, in transaction records required by law, or where it has been incorporated into another user's experience (for example, chat messages already received by other participants).

AR placements have their own lifecycle independent of account deletion (for example, Cloud Anchor placements expire after 365 days, and Supersize placements have a 60‑day approval window). Details are described in the App.

10. Your Rights and Choices

You have the following choices regarding your information.

  • Access and correction — You can review and edit profile information directly in the App.
  • Account deletion — You can delete your account from the in‑app settings screen. Account deletion cascades through your artworks, placements, and other associated records as described in §9.
  • Profile visibility — You can make your profile public or private from the in‑app settings. When set to private, other users opening your profile page will not see your biography, artwork list, or follower count. Your username and avatar still appear next to your published artworks and chat messages so that authorship can be attributed; following is also still available.
  • Notifications — You can disable push notifications globally or by category in the in‑app notification settings.
  • Location, camera, microphone, and photo library — You can grant or revoke these permissions at any time in your device settings; some features will be unavailable without them.
  • Advertising (ATT, iOS) — You can change your ATT choice at any time in iOS Settings. You can also enable "Limit Ad Tracking" or reset the IDFA from your device.
  • APPI rights — Subject to applicable conditions under the APPI, you may request disclosure, correction, suspension of use, or deletion of your personal information by contacting us at the address in §1. We may verify your identity before responding.

11. Security

We use industry‑standard administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect personal information, including encryption in transit (TLS), encryption at rest where supported by our providers, role‑based access control, and least‑privilege server configurations. No method of transmission or storage is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

If we become aware of a personal data incident that meets the notification threshold under applicable law, we will notify the relevant authority and affected users in accordance with that law.

12. Children

ARtEarth is rated 4+ on the App Store, but the App contains user‑generated content, AR camera use, and chat features that are most appropriate for users of sufficient age and maturity. The App is not directed to children under the age of 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided personal information without your consent, please contact us at the address in §1 and we will take steps to delete it.

We recommend that parents and guardians supervise children's use of AR applications, including ARtEarth.
